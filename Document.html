Phishing and cyber awareness:

Team Members have recently partaken in a new phishing cyber awareness program in order to understand what should be avoided and how to deal with a potential circumstance that would occur if a team member happens to fall for a scenario where private credentials are compromised.

Phishing scams illegally use the company name and logo. These scams target employees and customers with the goal to manipulate people into giving out private information.

These scams are represented in some of the following ways:

         Filling out a “customer satisfaction” survey and giving personal details

         Asking recipients to change an account password

         Other organisations making contact to “confirm” personal details

         Claiming a “reward” in a Bunnings branded email and filling in personal information such as zip codes, addresses or bank details in order to collect the reward
 

Bunnings is not associated with any of the above activities and does not use email or social media for any promotions offering free rewards such as gift cards. Bunnings will never ask for personal information, or banking details in unprofessional environments or through unsolicited communications. Bunnings works with an internal team to take action against these scams and encourages customers and employees to report suspicious activity and avoid putting any personal information in questionable sources without proper consultation with official sources.

How to identify a phishing scam:
Phishing scams are usually generic constructed emails or messages that do not correspond with official Bunnings messages. Phishing attacks create a sense of urgency in the attempts of trying to persuade the recipient into pressing the malicious link or attachments. Phishing attacks attempt to emulate official websites that require the recipient to input personal credentials such as usernames, passwords, addresses etc. Phishing scams use illegitimate website links that try to disguise themselves as real.

What to do if fallen for a phishing scam:

         Think carefully about the information that was provided to the fraudulent site.

         If you provided any banking or other financial details such as a credit card number, contact your financial institution immediately. Monitor and be mindful of suspicious activity on your account.

         If you provided any usernames or passwords, immediately change your passwords to a new and secure version. If you use the same password with multiple online services, change your password at those services too.

         Report the situation that has occurred with the internal bunnings cyber awareness team or report the cyber-crime at www.cyber.gov.au/acsc/report.
 

How to avoid phishing scams:

         Only enter information when personally prompted by internal sources such as managers etc

         Protect accounts with multi-factor authentication

         If something is questionable inform an official source to confirm authenticity of received email/link

         Be mindful of suspicious links and attachments.
